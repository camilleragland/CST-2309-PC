Shel Silverstein

Writer, poet, cartoonist, songwriter, playwright

Early Life

Shel Silverstein was born September 25, 1930, in Chicago, Illinois. He grew up in the Logan Square Chicago neighborhood, and later on, went to attend the University of Illinois. He was later expelled from the university for his poor grades and went on to go to the Chicago Academy of Fine Arts but then transferred to Roosevelt University to pursue a degree in English. However, in the middle of his studies, he was drafted into the US Army and served in Korea and Japan.

Career

While serving, Shel Silverstein became a journalist and cartoonist for the Stars and Stripes military newspaper. After being discharged he did some cartoons for Playboy Magazine in 1956 and went on to become the leading cartoonist for the company. After gaining some recognition for his cartoons, he started writing songs for country and rock stars, including most notably Johnny Cash. Some of Silverstein’s songs won Grammy awards, one of them being Cash’s song, “A Boy Named Sue”. Towards the middle of the 1980s, Silverstein's friend convinced him to do what he thought he would never do, become a children's writer. He soon became a popular children’s book author of all time, selling works such as Lafcadio, the Lion Who Shot Back, The Giving Tree, and Where the Sidewalk Ends.

Timeline of Accomplishments

  • Grammy Awards for Best Country Song, "A Boy Named Sue" in 1969
  • Grammy Awards for Best Children's Recording for Where the Sidewalk Ends in 1984
  • ALA Notable Book for Where the Sidewalk Ends in 1974
  • Outstanding Book Award for Where the Sidewalk Ends in 1974
  • Best Book Award in 1981 for A Light in the Attic
  • Children's Choice Award for The Missing Piece Meets the Big O in 1982
  • William Allen Book Award or A Light in the Attic in 1984

End of his Life

Silverstein won numerous awards for his work and became a well-known author for generations to come. He never married throughout his life, but he did have two children with two separate women. One of his children, Shoshannah, died of an aneurysm at the age of 11. He dedicated his poetry book Light in the Attic, to her. He later had a second child, Matthew, who is now a songwriter and Producer in New York. Shel Silverstein's life, unfortunately, ended at the age of 68, on May 10, 1999, when he had a heart attack at his home in Key West, Florida.
